# Hims & Hers - Side Effects & Safety Guide

## Common Side Effects by Treatment

### Oral Finasteride (Hair)
- **Common**: Decreased libido (1-2% of users), erectile dysfunction (1-2%), decreased ejaculate volume.
- **Resolution**: Most side effects resolve with continued use or upon discontinuation.
- **What to do**: If side effects persist beyond 2 weeks, message your provider through your account.

### Topical Minoxidil (Hair)
- **Common**: Scalp irritation, redness, dryness, initial shedding (weeks 2-6).
- **Less common**: Unwanted facial hair growth if product drips onto face.
- **Tip**: Apply carefully and wash hands after use to avoid transfer.

### Oral Minoxidil
- **Common**: Fluid retention (swelling in ankles/legs), increased heart rate, dizziness.
- **When to contact**: If you experience rapid heartbeat, significant swelling, or chest pain, contact your provider immediately.
- **Monitoring**: Your provider may check blood pressure during follow-ups.

### Spironolactone (Acne/Hair)
- **Common**: Dizziness, increased urination, breast tenderness, fatigue.
- **Serious**: Signs of high potassium (muscle weakness, irregular heartbeat) require immediate attention.

### Tretinoin (Skin)
- **Common**: Redness, peeling, dryness, photosensitivity.
- **Resolution**: These typically improve within 2-4 weeks as skin adjusts.
- **Management**: Use gentle cleanser, moisturizer, and SPF 30+ daily.

### Sertraline / Bupropion (Mental Health)
- **Common (Sertraline)**: Nausea, insomnia, drowsiness, dry mouth, sexual side effects.
- **Common (Bupropion)**: Dry mouth, insomnia, headache, nausea, appetite suppression.
- **Warning**: If you experience worsening depression, suicidal thoughts, or severe agitation, seek help immediately (call 988 for Suicide & Crisis Lifeline).

## When to Seek Emergency Care
Call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room if you experience:
- Difficulty breathing or swallowing
- Swelling of the face, lips, tongue, or throat
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe allergic reaction (hives, rash spreading rapidly)
- Seizures
- Thoughts of harming yourself or others

## When to Contact Your Provider
Message your provider through your account dashboard for:
- Side effects that persist beyond 2 weeks
- Moderate allergic reactions (mild rash)
- Questions about dosage adjustments
- Interactions with new medications

## Drug Interactions
Always inform your provider if you start any new medications, including:
- Prescription medications
- Over-the-counter drugs
- Herbal supplements (e.g., St. John's Wort, which interacts with SSRIs)
- Vitamins and minerals (especially potassium supplements with spironolactone)

## Alcohol and Treatment
- **Finasteride/Minoxidil**: Moderate alcohol consumption is generally safe but may increase dizziness.
- **Spironolactone**: Alcohol may increase dizziness and blood pressure effects.
- **Sertraline/Bupropion**: Alcohol can worsen side effects and reduce effectiveness. Limit or avoid alcohol.
- **General guidance**: Discuss alcohol use with your provider.
