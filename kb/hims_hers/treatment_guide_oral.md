# Hims & Hers - Treatment Guide: Oral Medications

## General Guidelines
- Take oral medications exactly as prescribed by your provider.
- Do not change your dosage without consulting a medical professional.
- Report any side effects promptly through your account dashboard.

## Finasteride Oral (Hims Hair)
- **Dosage**: Typically 1mg once daily.
- **Timing**: Can be taken with or without food. Consistent timing is recommended.
- **Course**: Continuous use required. Effects reverse within 6-12 months of stopping.
- **Important**: Women who are or may become pregnant should not handle crushed or broken tablets.

## Minoxidil Oral
- **Dosage**: As prescribed by your provider (typically 0.625mg - 2.5mg daily).
- **Timing**: Take with food to minimize potential side effects.
- **Note**: Oral minoxidil is a convenient alternative to topical for those who prefer not to apply daily.

## Spironolactone (Hers Acne & Hair)
- **Dosage**: Typically 25mg - 100mg daily as prescribed.
- **Hydration**: Drink plenty of water. Limit potassium-rich foods (bananas, spinach, avocados).
- **Monitoring**: Regular blood pressure and potassium level checks may be required.

## Sertraline (Hers Mental Health)
- **Dosage**: As prescribed. Typically starting at 25mg-50mg daily.
- **Titration**: Dose may be gradually increased based on your provider's guidance.
- **Timing**: Take consistently — morning or evening (if drowsiness occurs, try evening).
- **Onset**: Therapeutic effects may take 4-6 weeks to fully develop.
- **Discontinuation**: Do not stop abruptly. Taper under provider supervision.

## Bupropion (Hers Mental Health)
- **Dosage**: Typically 150mg once or twice daily as prescribed.
- **Timing**: Take at the same time each day. If twice daily, space doses 8+ hours apart.
- **Note**: Avoid taking late in the evening to prevent insomnia.

## Contraindications & Warnings
- Always inform your provider of all medications you are taking.
- Disclose any history of liver, kidney, or heart conditions.
- If you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding, consult your provider.
- Seek immediate medical attention for severe allergic reactions (rash, difficulty breathing, swelling).
